About Lifespark

Lifespark is dedicated to empowering individuals with lifesaving CPR knowledge and skills. Our mission is to make life-saving training accessible, modern, and engaging for everyone. Whether you're a parent, a student, or a concerned citizen, Lifespark provides clear, step-by-step resources to help you confidently respond in an emergency.

Our expert-led videos and guides are designed for all ages and backgrounds, making it simple to learn and practice CPR and emergency response techniques. Join our community, protect your loved ones, and help create a safer world—one heartbeat at a time.

CPR Training Library

Explore hands-on CPR techniques taught by experts. Each video is clear, practical, and ready when you need it most.

Basic CPR Essential
Size-Up
Before helping someone who is hurt, it’s important to stay calm and make sure the scene is safe for you. This video teaches how to quickly assess the environment for dangers like fire or unstable conditions. If the scene is safe, you’ll learn how to check if the person is responsive, how to call for help, and when to begin first aid or CPR.
Basic CPR Beginner
Initial Assessment
Once the scene is safe, the next step is to check if the person is breathing and has a pulse. This video explains how to identify signs of life, what to do if there’s no breathing or pulse, and how to spot visible injuries. It also covers how to speak to a conscious patient and begin a quick, effective check to prepare for first aid.
Basic CPR Beginner
Head-to-Toe Examination
A complete body check helps identify hidden injuries. This video walks you through how to carefully examine a person from head to toe—checking the head, eyes, chest, arms, abdomen, legs, and back. Learn how to look for signs like bleeding, swelling, or discomfort, and how to communicate gently with the patient throughout.
Basic CPR Beginner
Checking Vital Signs
Vital signs give important clues about a person's condition. In this video, you'll learn how to take a pulse, measure breathing rate, check skin temperature and color, and assess pupil reaction. These checks help determine how serious the situation is and whether the person’s condition is getting better or worse.
Basic CPR All Levels
Patient History (SAMPLE)
Asking the right questions helps responders provide better care. This video introduces the SAMPLE method—Signs, Allergies, Medications, Past history, Last food/drink, and Events leading to the emergency. You'll learn how to gather this key information and document it so it can be shared with doctors or EMTs.
Basic CPR Intermediate
Spinal Injury Check
If someone has fallen or been in a crash, their spine could be injured. This video shows how to carefully assess for spinal injuries without moving the person. You'll learn how to check for pain, movement, and feeling in their arms and legs, and when to keep them completely still until professional help arrives.
Basic CPR Essential
Choking Response
Choking can become life-threatening quickly. This video teaches how to recognize when someone is choking and how to perform the Heimlich maneuver. You'll learn the proper hand placement and motion, when to call 911, and what to do if the person becomes unconscious and stops breathing.
Basic CPR Essential
CPR Basics
When a person isn’t breathing and has no pulse, CPR can save their life. This video demonstrates how to perform chest compressions and rescue breaths effectively. Learn the proper technique, rhythm, and when to stop or continue based on the person’s response or the arrival of emergency help.
Basic CPR Essential
Using an AED
An Automated External Defibrillator (AED) can restart a heart during sudden cardiac arrest. In this video, you’ll learn how to turn on the AED, place the pads correctly, and follow the voice prompts safely. The video also covers how to combine AED use with CPR until emergency responders take over.
